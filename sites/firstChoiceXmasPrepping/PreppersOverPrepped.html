Overprepped: Beer and wine to go with your XMAS leftovers

No one is ever sad about having leftover Christmas ham, and it's the same for leftover Christmas drinks. Marry them together, add some cricket and you've got post-Christmas sorted. These are our picks for beer and wine that will pair magically with Christmas leftovers.

Leftover Seafood
How to store seafood properly

Seafood should be eaten as quickly as possible. But if you still have a few pieces left the next day: make sure it is well covered, never sitting in its own juice for any amount of time and placed in the coldest part of the fridge covered with ice - you want it to be between 0 - 4C. For specific advice, read this guide.

Beers and wine pairs

The saltiness of seafood calls for a sweet sidekick as a drink. If your preference is for a six-pack, go for beers with delicate fruity flavours like Sapporo or Thunder Road. For something a little more crisp and dry, grab some Hahn Super Dry.

If wine is more what you fancy, we recommend a dry Sparkling that has a touch of sweetness like Sorriso King Valley Prosecco - they'll be emphasised by the salty flavours of the food.

Leftover Chicken
How to store cooked chicken properly

Cooked chooks should be stored in the fridge - or in the freezer if more than two days' storage is required - immediately after steam has evaporated. Reheat at more than 70C for a minimum 2 minutes. For more details, read this guide.

Beers and wine pairs

The juiciness of a good cooked chook is best mates with aromatic and hoppy beers. Yenda Golden Ale has a nice balance of malt and hops with a crisp finish. But if you're after a more aromatic lager, try Coldstream's Czech Pilsner with Saaz hops from the motherland.

For wine lovers, different styles of cooking affect the flavours brought out in the chicken, and you want your wines to match. If your chicken was roasted, try a Pinot Noir like Curious Kiwi Pinot Noir. But if it was cooked on the barbie, reach for a spicier Shiraz like Two Churches Barossa Valley.

Leftover meat in a spicy curry
How to store properly

Cooked meat like roasts and barbecued meats should be refrigerated immediately. Your fridge should be around the 4 - 5C. When reheating, get the meat steaming hot at above 75C. Using up meat in a curry is a great way to make sure it gets eaten, and safely. Find out more about storing these meats.

Beer and wine pairs

When you've got a lot of leftover meat, it goes great when chucked in a spicy curry or Asian-style salad. This Pale Ale has a balanced bitterness that cuts through the heat of spicy Asian food. A pale ale like Yenda Pale Ale has a malt sweetness and medium bitterness that ups the tasty flavours of these foods.

While Coopers Sparkling Ale's fruity characters or an ice cold Miller Genuine Draft help cut through the spiciness.

Wine options for spicy foods include off-dry and medium sweet Rieslings like One Man's Journey Riesling or Barry and Sons Riesling. The fruity crisp flavours cleanse the palate between bites.

BBQs meats and burgers
How to store properly

Refrigerate cooked burger meat within two hours of being on the grill and wrap tightly with aluminum foil. Discard any minced meat which smells or looks even a bit off. Find out more about storing burger meat here.

Beer and wine pairs

Little Creatures Pale Ale is hop driven and bursting with flavour and aroma that is refreshing with a distinct bitterness. The big hop flavours pair well with beef burgers. An ice cold Coors or Heineken are the perfect lagers to cleanse the palate and bring out the delicious taste of BBQ classics such as burgers, wings and ribs.

Match the spiciness of a barbie with a richer wine like a full-bodied Cabernet Sauvignon - grilled meats will pull out the deep fruits and tannins of the wine. Our favourites include Punters Corner Cabernet Sauvignon and The Tutor Cabernet Sauvignon.

Lighter summer fare: sushi, salad and tacos

Turkey doesn't have a monopoly over meat at Christmas - as the chicken, ham and seafood will tell you. We've also got pairing options for foods better suited to the Aussie summer like sushi and tacos (which you've made from sliced up steaks from the barbie).

How to store properly

Store your salad in bowl in the fridge with paper towel over the top to catch the moisture, then wrap with cling wrap. For sushi, wring out excess moisture from the rolls then wrap tightly in plastic and eat within 24 hours.

Beer and wine pairs

Sushi needs delicate flavours in its drink accompaniments, so try its Japanese companion Kirin Megumi. For salads, turn to a crisp style lager with mild bitterness and fruit aroma of XXXX Summer Bright Lager - it goes particularly well with a Caesar salad. If you're turning your previously stored chicken or beef into tacos or burritos, go with the 'Chelada' style beer with natural lime flavours, like Miller Chill Premium.

If you'd rather a wine, these lighter foods should be paired with lighter-bodied wines like Chardonnay or a Rose. We recommend either the Two Churches Chardonnay or Barry and Sons Rose.
