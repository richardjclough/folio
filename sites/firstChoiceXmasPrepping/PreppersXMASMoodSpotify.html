Prepping the Christmas Mood

There's such a massive itinerary when it comes to prepping for Christmas Day. While you're so fixated on perfecting the bird, beers and bonbons, the tunes can often be left to an afterthought.

Setting the mood is so important when hosting, especially at Christmas, so don't leave your set list to a frantic scramble for the same-old carols on the day. You wouldn't serve your guests uncooked turkey - why serve up bland tunes?

So we've prepped three non-traditional playlists - guaranteed to get your crew in the mood, whoever they are.

Have yourself a modern little Christmas

While Mariah Carey's 1994 hit 'All I Want for Christmas' remains the last, universally accepted, modern Christmas classic, there have since been a few deserving candidates for the title. It's hard to believe Coldplay's 'Christmas Lights', Kylie Minogue's 'Every Day's Like Christmas' and Leona Lewis' 'One More Sleep' missed #1. All three tick off the Christmas classic checklist like Santa would check off his deliveries.

There are also some excellent Chrissy covers. Here's our list of (mostly) originals, all released after 2000, to give your Christmas a modern twist.

All I want for expats are tunes

Christmas is a time for being surrounded by family, but many expats and travellers don't have that luxury on the big day. That's why we've assembled a list of familiar classics, certain to make you feel a little bit closer to home and get your guests chanting along.

Stay classic this Christmas

Want to stick to the classics? From Bing Crosby's 'White Christmas' to Perry Como's 'It's Beginning to Look a Lot Like Christmas' to The Ronettes' 'Sleigh Ride', there are some Christmas songs that you can't get out of your head...and nor would you want to!
